# Track 1 Instructions: Exploring NF Tumor Data with cBioPortal

## Before You Start

Make sure you have a laptop with Chrome or Firefox open. No account or software is needed.

Navigate to: **[cbioportal.org](https://www.cbioportal.org)**

## Step 2: Explore the Study Summary

<!-- TODO: Add specific fields/numbers to call out from the chosen study -->

1. Review the summary page — note the number of patients, tumor types, and data types available
2. Click through the tabs at the top to see what data is available (mutations, copy number, clinical data, etc.)

---

## Step 3: Query for Genes of Interest

<!-- TODO: Confirm gene list relevant to the chosen study -->

1. Click **Query this study**
2. In the gene input box, enter the following genes: `NF1 NF2 CDKN2A TP53`
3. Make sure **Mutations** and **Copy number alterations** are checked
4. Click **Submit Query**

---

## Step 4: Interpret the OncoPrint

1. The OncoPrint shows each patient as a column and each gene as a row
2. Note which genes are most frequently altered
3. Look for patterns — do certain alterations co-occur or are they mutually exclusive?

## Step 5: Explore Additional Visualizations

Try the following from the results tabs:

- **Cancer Types Summary** — see how alterations are distributed across tumor subtypes
- **Mutations** — click into a gene to see individual mutation types and positions
- **Survival** — compare outcomes between patients with and without a given alteration (if clinical data is available)
